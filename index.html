About me

I believe that technology should serve public good. My work focuses on making digital systems work better for the institutions that serve citizens - especially in healthcare and public administration where the stakes are highest.

I'm interested in the intersection of policy and technology: how organizations can build resilient digital infrastructure, govern data responsibly, and navigate the complexity of institutional change. Whether working on healthcare interoperability, AI governance, or process automation, I'm driven by the challenge of solving real problems in how public institutions operate.

Currently based in Piedmont, I work on regional digital health initiatives. Beyond that, I contribute to open-source projects, engage with tech communities, and continue learning - both policy frameworks and technical systems architecture. I believe the most interesting problems require both perspectives.
